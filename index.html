Kingdom Phylum Class Order Family Genus Species

Individual capstone project: 3 week timeline. Fully traversable animal identification app displays information at every taxa level. Constructs pages from multiple API's and builds a cumulative, persistent user-answer object using CRUD, routing and file upload. Technologies: AngularJS, UIBootstrap, SCSS, Firebase.

I made this app because I love learning about animals, and I felt like a fully traversable list of animal taxa, with information about why the taxa were divided the way they were, was missing from the internet. I used the COL and EOL API's, which pull from over 100 other API's (mostly collegiate). They made the traversability of the taxa tree easy to work with, but I also researched and wrote hundreds of taxa questions in my Firebase database, to help describe the reasoning behind the taxa divisions- and in the process discovered that the sheer quantity of taxa is overwhelming, and the divides between them are often very small and hotly contested!

Deployed site currently under construction- it's going to need a back-end structure, which is coming soon.

Food Fight

Group project: 3 day timeline. Combat text-adventure game with composed player object, semi-random gameplay. Collaboration via GitHub and Trello. Participated in app design; wrote bonus.js and foodfight.js; contributed to all app files especially animations; drew player and enemy images. Technologies: JavaScript/jQuery

This early project helped solidify the idea of class inheritance and composition for me. It was also an exercise in team planning, and although I generated most of the content for this app, the thing I'm most proud of is the way our team worked together. For instance, I wrote the animations but a teammate wrote the Css; I wrote the gameplay functionality but a teammate wrote the 'checkfordeath' game-over functionality. I learned a lot about how to read other people's code and write my own code with other people's eyes in mind, during this project.

aMeowZon

Group project: 4 day timeline. CLI shopping app with Full TDD test suite, Curses menu display. Two database iterations. First: Pickle serialization of 5 related database files. Second: integration of SQL database. Contributed heavily to app structure; wrote cart, user, and payment modules; wrote shop, add-to-cart, and payment menus. Technologies: Python, SQL

Although this app is fairly simple to interface with, I fell in love with the way Python allowed me to plan and structure it in a clean, modular way. Especially once the concepts of unique ids and foreign/primary keys were introduced, the 'why' behind what we had been learning became clear- diagramming, planning, and explaining the relationships between the types of data became my favorite part of the project. I also got to work with some teammates who knew more about extra python packages, such as curser, and really enjoyed learning from them. We were able to take our project significantly above and beyond the original requirements- even including a fully traversable menu, one of my code obsessions.
